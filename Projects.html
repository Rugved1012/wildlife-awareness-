Wildlife Conservation Projects

Project Tiger

Launched in 1973 by the Government of India, Project Tiger aims to protect Bengal tigers and their habitats through anti-poaching measures, habitat restoration, and community involvement. It has led to the creation of several tiger reserves and a steady increase in tiger populations.

Amazon Rainforest Conservation

This initiative focuses on reducing deforestation, protecting indigenous communities, and preserving biodiversity in the Amazon rainforest. Strategies include afforestation, land-use planning, and the enforcement of environmental laws to maintain the ecological balance.

Great Barrier Reef Protection

The Great Barrier Reef faces threats from climate change, coral bleaching, and pollution. Conservation efforts include marine park regulations, research on coral restoration, pollution control programs, and climate change mitigation strategies to protect this vital marine ecosystem.

Snow Leopard Conservation

Snow leopards are endangered due to poaching and habitat fragmentation. Conservation programs focus on community education, anti-poaching patrols, habitat preservation, and international cooperation to ensure the survival of these elusive big cats in the high-altitude regions of Asia.

Save the Elephants Initiative

This project is dedicated to protecting elephants from poaching, habitat destruction, and human-elephant conflicts. Efforts include anti-poaching patrols, elephant corridors to reduce habitat fragmentation, and awareness programs to curb the illegal ivory trade.
