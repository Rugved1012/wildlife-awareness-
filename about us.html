Wildlife Conservation Organizations

Learn about the organizations working to protect wildlife and their habitats.

World Wildlife Fund (WWF)

Mission: WWF's mission is to stop the degradation of the planet's natural environment and build a future in which humans live in harmony with nature.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Protecting endangered species like tigers, elephants, and rhinos.
  • Addressing climate change and reducing human impact on the environment.
  • Promoting sustainable practices in businesses and industries.

Visit their website: www.worldwildlife.org

The Nature Conservancy

Mission: The Nature Conservancy works to conserve lands and waters on which all life depends, ensuring the protection of nature for future generations.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Habitat restoration for endangered species.
  • Protecting land and freshwater ecosystems.
  • Combating climate change by promoting sustainable land use.

Visit their website: www.nature.org

International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN)

Mission: IUCN is the global authority on the status of the natural world, providing data and promoting action to safeguard biodiversity and ecosystems.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Providing data on species conservation status (IUCN Red List).
  • Promoting sustainable use of natural resources.
  • Encouraging international conservation efforts.

Visit their website: www.iucn.org

Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS)

Mission: WCS works globally to conserve wildlife and wild places through science, advocacy, education, and fieldwork.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Protecting wildlife habitats and ecosystems.
  • Educating the public on wildlife conservation.
  • Combating illegal wildlife trade and poaching.

Visit their website: www.wcs.org

Wildlife Protection Society of India (WPSI)

Mission: WPSI is dedicated to protecting endangered species and combating poaching in India. They work on field-based conservation and advocacy efforts.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Anti-poaching operations.
  • Training wildlife protection staff.
  • Advocacy for better wildlife protection laws.

Visit their website: www.wpsi-india.org

Defenders of Wildlife

Mission: Defenders of Wildlife focuses on protecting native animals and plants in the U.S. They advocate for wildlife-friendly policies and actions.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Protecting endangered species in the U.S.
  • Advocating for wildlife-friendly legislation.
  • Ensuring safe wildlife corridors across landscapes.

Visit their website: www.defenders.org

The Dian Fossey Gorilla Fund

Mission: The Dian Fossey Gorilla Fund is focused on the protection and conservation of mountain gorillas, with fieldwork and research in Rwanda and the Congo.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Gorilla research and monitoring.
  • Community engagement to reduce human-wildlife conflict.
  • Anti-poaching and habitat protection for gorillas.

Visit their website: www.gorillafund.org

Born Free Foundation

Mission: Born Free works to ensure that wild animals are protected and live freely in their natural habitats, away from captivity and exploitation.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Ending the captivity of wild animals.
  • Protection of animals through direct conservation projects.
  • Advocacy for wildlife-friendly policies.

Visit their website: www.bornfree.org.uk

Save the Elephants

Mission: Save the Elephants focuses on elephant conservation and aims to protect elephants through research and anti-poaching efforts in Africa.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Elephant monitoring and conservation.
  • Combating poaching and the ivory trade.
  • Policy advocacy to protect elephant habitats.

Visit their website: www.savetheelephants.org

The David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust

Mission: The David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust works to protect wildlife, particularly elephants, and rehabilitate orphans back into the wild.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Elephant rescue and rehabilitation.
  • Anti-poaching and habitat protection.
  • Community outreach to reduce human-wildlife conflict.

Visit their website: www.sheldrickwildlifetrust.org
